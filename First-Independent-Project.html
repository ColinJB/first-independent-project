Who am I?

My name is Colin, which I'm assuming you know at this point. I consider my hometown to be Atlanta, since that is where I spent the majority of my life. I was born in Park Ridge, Illinois; although we moved while I was still an infant. From there, my family and I moved to Maryland, then Georgia, then Massachusetts, back to Georgia, down to Orlando, and the finally back to good ole' Georgia again. My father is in the hotel business, hence the constant relocations. My pops is in sales; always has been. I followed his foot steps when I was out of high school. I've held a few different sales positions, and it really comes naturally to me. It taught me a lot about business and the sales process in general. However, I have a greater vision of what I would like to accomplish in my lifetime. Money is not my motivation. I hope to learn the skills necessary that will enable me to create a platform of positive and tangible change within the world.

Background

I graduated from Etowah High School in 2012. As I stated previously, I jumped head first into the sales realm of business; and excelled. The only significant education I received other than that was my attendance of the National Youth Leadership Forum on Medicine at Emory University. I was invited based off of my performance on standardized tests and my interest in the medical field. I have strayed away from the medical field for a couple of reasons. My interest in technology for one. Two, my interest in business. Three, and most importantly, my desire to possess the skills necessary to positively influence the world in as large a way as possible.

Interests

My largest interest is personal growth. I hope to backpack the world and experience as many different cultures as I can. I once told myself, that I would like to experience anything that brings joy to anyone. Not as easy as it sounds. Sometimes the things that bring the greatest joy, also elicit the greatest fear.

Such is life. However, on a slightly less abstract note, here are some of the things I enjoy right now:

Cooking. For myself, but especially others. Few things engage all the senses so vividly. Plus, the satisfaction of seeing the smiles and receiving the gratitude of friends and loved ones when you feed them a delicious meal, is second to none. Who doesn't like some good grub?

Outdoors. My mother bought me a shirt that says "Nature is cheaper than therapy"; and lords knows that is true. In such trying times as these, we could all afford to gain some of the peace and clarity that Mother Nature so heartily offers.

Basketball. I'm not a big fan of really any other sport. I grew up with my best friend aiming to join the NBA. Between growing up and shooting hoops constantly with him, to watching him climb the ranks and play for a Division 1 school; basketball definitely holds a place in my heart.

Philosophy and Politics. Few things intrigue me more than Philosophy and Politics; in that order. If the world would consider them together more often, I think we might all be in a better place.
